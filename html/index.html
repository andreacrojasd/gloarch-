Globalization has had a profound impact on the pace and rate of change in urban spaces, leading to drastic transformations over short periods of time. As the world becomes more interconnected, cities are experiencing rapid development and urbanization driven by economic and cultural factors. This has resulted in the demolition of historic buildings and the construction of modern structures, altering the architectural fabric of urban areas. The need for functionality and efficiency often outweighs the preservation of cultural heritage, leading to the loss of unique architectural features and the homogenization of urban landscapes. The influence of globalization in architecture has created a tension between the desire for progress and the preservation of a city's identity and history.
